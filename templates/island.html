Island

The cottage, located on a small private island just offshore (100 yards) from the main property, was built in the early 20th century. The island is approximately an acre in size and has a network of narrow trails.

The cottage has accommodations for a family of up to six persons. Furnishings are simple but adequate. The cottage is equipped with blankets, but family and friends are expected to provide their own sheets and towels.

The living room is of moderate size with a stone fireplace and a picture window to the northwest with an expansive view of the lake and the mountains beyond. The dining room has a large bay window with the dining area virtually surrounded by woods. The kitchen has an electric stove, running hot and cold water and an electric refrigerator; it is fully equipped with tableware and utensils.

There is a large master bedroom with its own fireplace and a wash basin in an alcove with hot and cold running water; two small bedrooms (one with wash basin) with one twin bed each and a large screened sleeping porch that accommodates up to four twin beds.

Porches extend around three sides of the house. There is an inside shower and an indoor toilet. Garbage must be taken to the main road for municipal collection service.

A rowboat and a canoe go with the cottage. In addition to the wharf on the island, there is a mainland wharf reserved for use with the island, with a path to the parking area off the Mott road.

Electricity and fireplace wood are included in the usage fee, and a telephone is available. The telephone number is (450) 228-8531, and address is:
680 rue Daviault, Entrelacs, Quebec, Canada JOT 2EO.
